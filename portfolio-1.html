Student Performance Visualization

Data Source: Kaggle.com

Data Explorartion / EDA

I have used the data from website Kaggle to visualize the Studnets' exam (reading, writing, math) score based on the data of each student's status such as "Did he/she have lunch before the test?", "Parent's education level", "Has he/she completed test preparation course?", and "Race/Ethnicity". The main point of this project was to see the relationship between the features above and the test scores.

Graph above shows the distribution of the student for each feature. Y axis of each graph represents the number of students. For the gender feature, I was able to see that data is well balanced. Arrested Date

From the bar graph above, the amount of crime had been constantly decreasing since 2011. Probably because of the development of technology and science. The more developed technology, the more probability that police can catch crimes and easier to prevent the crimes with Cameras for example.

Law Category

When we looked at the "Folium" map, there were only blue and orange circles and this graph supports the visualization. In the graph, the number of felony and misdemeanor are significantly higher than the other type of crimes. Interesting part is that there is really small amount of crime occurrences of violation and infraction while the number of felony crimes is pretty much consistent. The crime of misdemeanor has still the highest, but it had been consistently decreasing.

Race / Gender

The graph also illustrated the difference of the crime rate between the races and genders. Black people tend to commit more crimes than that of other races, and men tend to commit more crimes than that of female.

Age

Most of the observaction can be seen as obvious, but there is also an interesting observation. There are few people who are older than 65 still committing crimes. It is still quite small amount of numbers compared to other age groups, but still it was an interesting data for me to observe.
