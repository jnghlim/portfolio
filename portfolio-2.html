Crime Rate Visualization

Data Source: Kaggle.com | Library: Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Folium

Data Explorartion / EDA

I have used the data from website Kaggle to visualize the Crime rate in New York City based on the data of each occurrence of the crime. After checking up with the data, I cleansed my unorganized data (categorical values with unreadable letters, abbreviations), using pandas library (used mapping and renaming).

After data cleansing, I used "Folium" library from python to visualize the data points, dividing up the colors for each community districts (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Bronx, Queens, Staten Island).

    Navy Circle: Misdemeanor
    Orange Circle: Felony
    Green Circle: Violation
    Red Circle: Infraction

Below button is connected to a page where you can zoom in and out or move around the map.

Folium Map

From the Folium map, the colors are mostly navys and oranges which are misdemeanor and felony. If you go into the link and zoom in few red and green circles are noticed also, but there are not as many as the navy and orange circles.

Now, I visualized the data by "Date", "Law Category", "Race/Gender", and "Age"

Arrested Date

From the bar graph above, the amount of crime had been constantly decreasing since 2011. Probably because of the development of technology and science. The more developed technology, the more probability that police can catch crimes and easier to prevent the crimes with Cameras for example.

Law Category

When we looked at the "Folium" map, there were only blue and orange circles and this graph supports the visualization. In the graph, the number of felony and misdemeanor are significantly higher than the other type of crimes. Interesting part is that there is really small amount of crime occurrences of violation and infraction while the number of felony crimes is pretty much consistent. The crime of misdemeanor has still the highest, but it had been consistently decreasing.

Race / Gender

The graph also illustrated the difference of the crime rate between the races and genders. Black people tend to commit more crimes than that of other races, and men tend to commit more crimes than that of female.

Age

Most of the observaction can be seen as obvious, but there is also an interesting observation. There are few people who are older than 65 still committing crimes. It is still quite small amount of numbers compared to other age groups, but still it was an interesting data for me to observe.

After looking at the crime rates visualization for each categorical feature, I visualized the crime rate for each community district which are Manhattan, Queens, Staten Island, Brooklyn, and Bronx. After that, I counted the number of crime occurrences for each section code within the community district and listed which place is safe to visit and which place is not safe to visit.

Among all the continuous features, I have decided to pick one feature which was "age" to plot a histogram to see the distribution. Below illustrates the histogram and statistic of "age" feature.

I have excluded the 20s' heart attack rate because there was not enough data. The amount of data is unbalanced between the (30s-60s) and (30s and 70s). Just by looking at the numbers of "more chance of heart attack", 50s have the highest, but they have the highest number of "less chance of heart attack" at the same time. With the statistic, I was able to conclude that the most vulnerable age range was surprisingly 40s.

Prediction

Above picture is a scatter plot of the two most important features (Highly contributes to predict the output) of this data which are "thalachh", and "oldpeak". As you can see that data is hard to be separated linearly. Therefore, I assume the models that use linear method won't perform well.

Above Dataframe illustrates the each model's accuracy scores and like I have assumed before, the methods that use linear method did not perform well, but KNN model performed the best instead.

ROC Curve of the Best Model (KNN)

The ROC Curve shows the trade-off between sensitivity and specificity. If the model gives the curves closer to the top-left corner, it indicates a better performance. On the other hand, if the model gives the curves closer to the 45 degree diagonal line, it represents the less accuracy.

KNN Visualization

In order to visualize the KNN classification, I had to choose two features that are correlated the least and which both correlates the most to the output which are "cp", "oldpeak". (Referred to the heatmap below.)As a result, KNN Classification with only two features gave us 80% of accuracy which is pretty good.
