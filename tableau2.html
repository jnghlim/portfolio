Marketing Campaign A/B Test

Data Source: Kaggle.com | Language: Python(Pandas), SQL | Viz: Tableau

About the Data and Goal of this Visualization

Observing how the amount of customer purchases and spends money differs by the campaign which each customer accepts. Therefore, we can decide which campaign was more effective and which campaign fits the best for certain groups.

Preparation

  • Downloaded Excel data from Kaggle.com
  • Done ETL with SQL
  • Saved modified version of data as an Excel file and used it for Tableau Visualization

Story (Tableau Public)

How Total Products Purchased and Total Money Spent differs by Campaign overall

  • By looking at this dashboard, we can see that the amount of customer purchases and spend money for Treatment campaign is higher than that of the Control Campaign. This applies to every category of the products(Fish, Fruits, Gold, Meat, Sweet, Wines), and every way how customer purchases the products(Catalog, Store, Web).

  • However, the insights we can get from visualziation differs by the income group. Divided income group approximately which was referred by the website "World Bank Blogs" - New World Bank country classifications by income level: 2021-2022

  • Differs by Income Group
    • Low Income

      • Among the Low income group, people who are exposed to Treatment Campaign tend to purchase and spend money more than that of people who are exposed to Control Campaign

      For Low income group, Treatment Campaign performed better.

    • Middle Income

      • Middle income group has an even distribution between the two Campaigns and has some categories that Control Campaign has higher amount. (Total amount of Purchases, Wines - Product Category, Store - Purchase Method)

      For Middle income group, Control Campaign also has significant amount of purchases and money spent by customers, compared to other income groups.

      There was only a slight difference where the categories that Control Campaign affected more than that of Treatment Campaign. It is hard to conclude that one specific Campaign affected Middle income group the most overall.

    • High Income

      • High income group has simple and interesting insights. There was no one who purchased or spent money that are exposed from Control Campaign.

      Probably because there was not enough data points of people who are in High income range. The amount of purchases and money spent by customers differ a lot from other income groups.

  • Differs by Company Website Visit Frequency
    • Frenquency 0-3

    • Frenquency 3-6

    • Frenquency 6-9

    • Frenquency 9-12

      • For this condition, it was interesting to see how the amount of influence that Control Campaign affects to the customer consistently decrease as the frequency of website visit increases.

      Another interesting insight is that significant amount of money customer spent for Control Campaign decreased for all of the product categories except for the "Wine" cateogry until the frequency of website visit reached 9.

  • Differs by Age

    • This dashboard gives us an interesting insights. There is only one single age group(50s) who are more affected by the Control Treatment.

    • Age group of 70s have slightly more affected by the Control Campaign for total money spent from Customers, and slightly more affected by the Treatment Campaign for Total Purchases

  • Differs by Marital Status

    • This dashboard also gives us an interesting insights like above. All the groups of people who are single(Absurd, Alone, Divorced, Widow, YOLO) were affected mostly by the Treatment Campaign while the married group is more affected by the control group.

    • It indicates that Treatment Campaign fits better for the single group and Control Campaign fits better for the couple group.

Perferred Target groups for Control Campaign

  • Age Group - 50s
  • Married Group
